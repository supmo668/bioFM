\subsection{Agents genuinely use their configuration freedom}

Across 108 lifecycle runs spanning 36 held-out tasks,
the Architect's backbone-choice entropy is
$\mathbf{ 0.36 }$ nats
($\ln(3)\!\approx\!1.10$ is the uniform-3-choice ceiling), and the
HVG-count entropy is 0.30 nats. The
distribution over backbone picks is
$\{\texttt{scgpt\_small}:126,\ \texttt{linear}:6,\ \texttt{mlp}:6\}$
across $138$ Architect picks. This answers the paper's v0.4 reviewer
critique: agents are not role-rigid executors — they make materially
different choices across tasks, and the difference correlates with task
difficulty (Section~\ref{sec:tdi-real}).

\subsection{Cross-dataset transfer}

Calibrating TDI on Adamson and applying the fixed coefficients to
Norman's traces yields Spearman $\rho\!=\!n/a$ — a direct
check that the metric isn't overfit to one dataset. Pre-registered gate:
$\rho\!>\!0.4$.
